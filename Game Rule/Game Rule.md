4. Quantum Actions
A) Split

A piece moves to two squares simultaneously.

Example: A knight in superposition could occupy b1 and c3 at the same time.

Cost: Using Split ends your turn.

Rules for Split:

The target squares must be legal destinations for that piece in classical chess.

The piece now exists as a superposition across those two squares.

B) Entangle

Entanglement links pieces so that they must collapse in correlated ways.

When does entanglement happen?

When a piece moves through (not stops on) a square occupied by another piece

When a split moving piece interacts with a target square occupied by another superposed piece

How entanglement works:

The pieces now share a quantum state.

If one is measured (collapses), the linked piece may also collapse or adjust according to the entanglement rule.

C) Measure

Measurement collapses superposition/entanglement to a definite state.

8. Check and Checkmate

A king in superposition can be in two squares.

A king is in check if any of its possible squares is attacked.

Checkmate happens when all superposed positions of the king cannot escape check.
